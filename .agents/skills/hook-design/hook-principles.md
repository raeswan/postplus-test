# Hook Principles

Use this reference when the hook needs stronger structure, clearer specificity, better product timing, or a cleaner handoff to downstream work.

## Core Idea

Do not treat hooks as a swipe-file problem.

Treat them as a mechanism problem:
- what makes the viewer stop
- why that mechanism fits this audience state
- when the product is allowed to enter
- what failure signal would tell us the hook is not working

## Product Reveal Timing

Do not introduce the product before the hook has created enough curiosity, proof, or self-recognition to earn it.

Default rule:
- keep the product out of the opening when early product presence would trigger ad detection
- reveal the product only after the problem, result, or desire already feels real
- make the product look like the conclusion to the scene, not the premise

Useful check:
- if frame one or the opening line makes the viewer think "this is an ad," the reveal is probably too early

Exception:
- if the hook mechanism is `proofFirst`, the product may appear early, but only as supporting evidence, not as the hero object

## Specificity Beats Generic Wording

Hook specificity is usually what separates generic low-recognition openings from strong self-identification.

Stronger:
- `you've been trying to lose weight for 8 months and the scale hasn't moved`

Weaker:
- `trying to lose weight?`

When upgrading a weak hook, try to add:
- duration
- stalled effort
- visible frustration
- a familiar failed pattern
- one concrete life context

## Hook Mechanisms

These are reusable mechanisms, not rigid templates.

### `boldClaim`

Use when the brand challenges a default belief and the viewer needs a reason to reassess.

### `questionGap`

Use when the audience has a real unresolved question and the answer can be delivered quickly.

### `proofFirst`

Use when trust is the main barrier and visible evidence can appear early.

### `painRecognition`

Use when the audience already feels the pain and the strongest stop-scroll comes from self-recognition.

### `patternInterrupt`

Use when feed sameness is the problem and the first second should feel unexpected.

### `socialOrIdentityStake`

Use when the real pain is embarrassment, status loss, dating, confidence, or presentation.

## Working Structure

A reliable baseline structure is:

```text
Hook -> listener reaction -> problem deepening -> product as natural conclusion -> CTA
```

## Diagnostic Signals

Use these as review signals, not universal KPIs:
- the first 1-3 seconds make the chosen mechanism legible
- the viewer question is clear without a long explanation
- the body pays off the opening by the next few beats
- the product does not trigger obvious ad detection too early
- the promise of the opening matches what the video actually delivers

## Common Failure Patterns

- product-first opening with no earned curiosity
- generic pain language that fits everyone and therefore no one
- listener reaction that sounds theatrical instead of native
- product reveal with no problem-deepening beat
- punchy wording with no real viewer question underneath
- a good mechanism in theory that disappears in frame one
